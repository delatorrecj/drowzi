# Project Initialization: Habit-Gated Wake-Up App (INIT.MD)

## 1. Brand Core & Identity

### 1.1 Brand Personality
* **Brand Archetype:** The Challenger / The Personal Trainer.
* **Tone of Voice:** Energetic, uncompromising, slightly cheeky, and deeply encouraging.
* **Core Values:** Discipline, vitality, gamification, and momentum.

### 1.2 Color Palette & Psychology
* **Primary Color - Awakening Yellow (#F4C430):** High energy, alertness, and optimism. Used for app backgrounds, mascot primary color, and success screens.
* **Secondary Color - Grounded Brown (#654321):** Earthy and reliable. Provides a high-contrast, easy-on-the-eyes backdrop for early mornings. Used for text, nav bars, and the mascot's lower body.
* **Action Color - Adrenaline Red / Pulse Orange (#E63946 / #FF5A5F):** Denotes urgency and physical action. Used for "Alarm Active" screens and required task prompts.

### 1.3 Typography
* **Headers & Numbers:** Thick, bold, and condensed sans-serif (e.g., *Impact, Roboto Condensed, Montserrat ExtraBold*) for a commanding, sports-brand feel.
* **Body Copy:** Clean, highly legible sans-serif (e.g., *Inter, Poppins*) to reduce eye strain for sleepy users.

### 1.4 Mascot & Visuals
* **Concept:** Inspired by the Drowzee pixel art. An inversion of the traditional "dream eater" – this mascot "eats" grogginess and actively fights sleep.
* **Evolution:** Starts sleepy/demanding during the alarm phase, becoming increasingly energetic and "pumped up" as the user completes their habit toll.

## 2. Problem-Solution Fit

### 2.1 The Problem: The Intention-Behavior Gap in Morning Routines
* **The Pain Point:** People want to establish healthy morning routines (reading, meditating, exercising), but sleep inertia destroys willpower at 6:00 AM, leading to chronic snoozing and an immediate sense of failure.
* **The Target Audience:** Chronic snoozers, productivity seekers, habit-builders, and anyone struggling to transition from sleeping to an active, productive state.
* **Failure of Current Alternatives:** Traditional alarms and "puzzle alarms" only engage short-term memory or allow mindless swiping. They fail to enforce actual, meaningful lifestyle habits.

### 2.2 The Solution: Modular Habit-Gated Alarms
* **The Fix:** A strict, customizable habit enforcer. The alarm will not disarm until the phone’s sensors or camera verify a user-selected habit is complete.
* **Habit Categories (Onboarding Selection):**
    * *Physical:* 10 push-ups, 30 seconds of stretching, or yoga poses.
    * *Environmental:* Scanning the barcode of a coffee bag in the kitchen or toothpaste in the bathroom.
    * *Cognitive/Mindfulness:* Reading a motivational passage aloud (voice recognition) or holding a meditation pose.
* **The Psychological Hook:** Links the unavoidable urgency of an alarm to a desired personal goal. Users defeat their grogginess by successfully executing the routine they’ve been trying to build.

## 3. Quantitative Data & Market Validation

* **The Power of Habit Stacking:** Behavioral psychology heavily supports tying a new habit to an established, unavoidable cue. By forcing the new habit to act as the *off switch* for the morning alarm, the app digitally enforces this core behavioral principle.
* **Environmental Context Shifts Defeat Sleep Inertia:** Studies in chronobiology show that changing physical environments (e.g., forcing a user to walk to the bathroom to scan a barcode) rapidly signals to the circadian system that the sleep period is over, drastically reducing the urge to return to bed.
* **Micro-Commitments Drive Long-Term Retention:** Forcing large goals often leads to app abandonment. Focusing on "micro-habits" (drinking water, scanning an item, a few stretches) yields significantly higher compliance. Lower-barrier tasks reduce churn while still effectively waking the user up.
