# Drowzi — Slide Deck

> **Format guidance:** One idea per slide. Presenter notes are in blockquotes under each slide. Slides marked `[DEMO]` require a live phone screen or recorded video clip. Keep animations to slide-in transitions only — no builds within a slide.

---

## Slide 01 — The Hook

**Visual:** Full bleed. Dark background. A blurry, groggy bedroom at 6:00 AM. Three alarm notifications stacked on a phone screen. All snoozed.

**Headline (big, centered):**
> You set 3 alarms.
> You slept through all of them.

**No subtext. No logo yet.**

> **Presenter note:** Open with silence. Let the image sit for 2–3 seconds before speaking. The audience will recognize themselves instantly. Say: "This is every morning for most of us."

---

## Slide 02 — Name the Enemy

**Visual:** Single word, full slide. Dark brown background. Yellow text.

**Headline:**
> Sleep inertia.

**One-line below:**
> The physiological grogginess that destroys willpower at exactly 6 AM.

> **Presenter note:** Give this a name so the audience can hate it. Explain briefly: "Your brain is still in a low-arousal state for 15–30 minutes after waking. Willpower is offline. That's why intentions made the night before collapse the moment the alarm goes off."

## Slide 03 — Current Solutions Fail

**Visual:** Three icons in a row — a math puzzle, a swipe gesture, a QR code on a nightstand. Each with a subtle red X.

**Headline:**
> Puzzle alarms don't work either.

**Three short labels under the icons:**
- Solve a math problem → back asleep in 2 minutes
- Swipe to dismiss → mindless muscle memory
- Scan a code → the same 5 seconds, every day

> **Presenter note:** "The category of 'hard to dismiss alarms' exists. But every existing solution only engages short-term memory or motor reflex — trivial effort. None of them enforce an actual habit."

---

## Slide 04 — The Insight

**Visual:** Bold typographic slide. Yellow background. Brown text.

**Headline:**
> The habit should be the off-switch.

**One line below:**
> Not the reward. Not the reminder. The actual, unavoidable off-switch.

> **Presenter note:** This is the core thesis. Pause after saying it. Let it land. "What if you could not turn off your alarm without doing the thing you've been trying to build?"

## Slide 06 — DEMO: Motion Gate `[DEMO]`

**Visual:** Phone mockup, full screen — alarm-active screen showing Drowzi mascot angry, red pulsing background, alarm audio implied.

**Headline (above mockup):**
> Scenario 1: Push-ups.

**Demo script (presenter speaks, not on slide):**
1. Alarm fires — red screen, mascot is furious, audio blaring
2. Tap "Start Push-Ups"
3. Camera opens — rep counter at 0/10
4. Do push-ups — counter increments live: 3… 6… 9… 10
5. Alarm stops. Yellow screen. Mascot celebrates.

**On-slide caption (small, bottom):**
> Camera counts every rep. Alarm stops at 10. Not before.

> **Presenter note:** Let the demo breathe. Don't narrate every rep — the visual does the work. The silence of the alarm stopping is the moment.

## Slide 09 — The Science (in 3 bullets)

**Visual:** Clean dark slide. Yellow accent line. No stock photos.

**Headline:**
> This isn't willpower. It's architecture.

**Three lines (no bullet points — just stacked text):**

`Habit stacking` — Tying a new behavior to an unavoidable daily cue locks it in.

`Environmental shift` — Moving your body to a new space defeats sleep inertia faster than any alarm sound.

`Micro-commitments` — Small, completable habits build streaks. Streaks build identity.

> **Presenter note:** "We didn't invent this. Behavioral psychology and chronobiology did. We just put it in an app."

## Slide Notes — Best Practice Reminders

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| One idea per slide | Audiences read OR listen — never both |
| Demo early (slides 6–8) | Don't make them wait 20 slides to see the thing |
| No bullet walls | If it needs bullets, it needs a conversation, not a slide |
| Silence the alarm live | The moment the alarm stops in the demo IS the pitch — let it breathe |
| End on the hook, not on "thanks" | The last emotion should be the brand emotion: challenged, ready |
| Presenter notes are for you | Nothing on a slide that you could just say out loud |
